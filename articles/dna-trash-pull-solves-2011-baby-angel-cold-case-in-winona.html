DNA Trash Pull Solves 2011 'Baby Angel' Cold Case in Winona

A covert DNA extraction and genetic genealogy led investigators to the biological mother 13 years after the newborn was found in the Mississippi River.

Crime Docket March 24, 2026 588 words · 3 min read

The covert extraction of familial DNA from discarded trash has resulted in second-degree manslaughter charges against a 43-year-old woman, concluding a 13-year investigation into the death of an unidentified newborn. Known locally as the “Baby Angel” cold case, the breakthrough relied on investigative genetic genealogy (the use of commercial and forensic DNA databases to track familial lineage) facilitated by the Minnesota Bureau of Criminal Apprehension (BCA) and a specialized forensics nonprofit.

Forensic Pathology and the 2011 Discovery

On September 5, 2011, boaters discovered the body of a 7-pound female newborn floating in the Mississippi River in Homer, Minnesota, just south of Winona. According to crime scene reports, the infant was wrapped in a green T-shirt, encased in two brown plastic garbage bags, and placed inside a canvas tourist tote bag.

Investigators recovered several artifacts alongside the body, including incense, a distinctive blue pendant featuring an eye pattern, and porcelain glass angel figurines. These figurines led the Winona community to dub the unidentified victim “Baby Angel.”

According to the criminal complaint filed in Winona County District Court, an updated forensic autopsy determined the infant was born alive, noting the umbilical cord had not been clamped or cut by a medical provider. The medical examiner concluded the cause of death was blunt force trauma, which resulted in multiple skull fractures and brain hemorrhaging. Pathologists noted these injuries were inflicted while the infant was alive and that the resulting brain bleeding could have been medically treated to decrease the likelihood of death.

Investigative Genetic Genealogy and the DNA Trash Pull

The case remained unsolved until March 2023, when the Winona County Sheriff’s Office and the BCA partnered with the Firebird Forensics Group. Utilizing crime scene DNA, the group mapped familial lineages and identified Winona resident Jennifer Nichole Baechle as a highly probable suspect.

According to court documents, investigators approached Baechle in mid-2023 to request a voluntary buccal (cheek) swab. Baechle declined the request and retained legal counsel. To circumvent the refusal, investigators executed a covert “trash pull” outside Baechle’s residence, recovering a discarded feminine hygiene product.

The BCA laboratory extracted DNA from the item, confirming it matched the biological mother of the infant. Armed with this comparative data, authorities secured a search warrant to compel a direct DNA sample from Baechle. Laboratory analysis confirmed with “very strong evidence” that Baechle was the biological mother and that her DNA matched genetic material found on items inside the canvas bag.

Second-Degree Manslaughter Charges

In April 2025, Baechle was arrested and charged with two counts of second-degree manslaughter. WKOW reports that bail was set at $200,000 without conditions, or $20,000 with conditions including GPS monitoring.

Further corroborating the forensic findings, witness interviews detailed in the criminal complaint indicate Baechle was living in a van in Winona during the fall of 2011, isolated from her family. Relatives also confirmed she was an avid collector of angel figurines and positively identified the blue pendant found at the crime scene as belonging to her.

The Evidence

  • The Victim: A full-term, 7-pound female newborn discovered in the Mississippi River in September 2011.
  • Cause of Death: Blunt force trauma resulting in multiple skull fractures and brain hemorrhaging, sustained post-birth.
  • Forensic Link: A covert DNA extraction from a discarded hygiene product provided the genetic match necessary to secure a search warrant for the suspect’s direct DNA.
  • Physical Evidence: DNA found on items inside the canvas bag matched the suspect; relatives positively identified the blue pendant and angel figurines found with the body.
