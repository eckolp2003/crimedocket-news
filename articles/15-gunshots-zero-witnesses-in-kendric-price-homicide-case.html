15 Gunshots, Zero Witnesses in Kendric Price Homicide Case

Boston Police Department detectives continue investigating the 2019 Dorchester shooting, facing a severe lack of witness cooperation despite recovering evidence of up to 15 fired rounds.

Crime Docket March 25, 2026 476 words · 2 min read

A barrage of up to 15 gunshots and the immediate dispersal of a large crowd left behind a complex crime scene on Greenwood Street, where 32-year-old Kendric Price sustained fatal ballistic trauma. Seven years after the March 2, 2019 shooting, the Boston Police Department (BPD) Homicide Unit classifies the Kendric Price homicide as an open and active investigation. The case remains severely hindered by a persistent lack of witness cooperation, despite the high number of individuals present at the time the firearm was discharged.

Crime Scene Analysis at Greenwood Street

At approximately 2:58 a.m., BPD officers responded to reports of gunfire near the intersection of Greenwood and Ellington Streets in Dorchester, Massachusetts. Upon arrival at an attached garage at 12/12A Greenwood Street, first responders located Price suffering from multiple gunshot wounds. He was pronounced dead at the scene, and the Office of the Chief Medical Examiner officially ruled the death a homicide following a standard autopsy.

Investigators determined that a large, late-night gathering was taking place at the location prior to the shooting. Acoustic data from neighborhood reports indicated up to 15 shots were fired in rapid succession. Price was found wearing his standard attire—jeans, sneakers, and a checkered shirt—yielding no immediate investigative anomalies regarding his presence at the gathering.

Obstacles in the Dorchester Unsolved Murder

The primary investigative hurdle remains the complete absence of cooperative eyewitnesses. BPD spokesman John Boyle recently confirmed the investigation’s active status, noting that the crowd scattered immediately following the gunfire.

The rapid dispersal of attendees complicates both the timeline and the preservation of trace evidence (microscopic physical evidence such as fibers or DNA), as the chaotic exit likely contaminated the immediate boundaries of the crime scene. The “wall of silence” from potential witnesses limits investigators’ ability to contextualize the recovered ballistic evidence or establish a definitive line of sight for the shooter. Law enforcement officials have not publicly named a suspect or established a clear motive, though family representatives have firmly stated on the record that Price, a former collegiate athlete and local youth mentor, had no documented gang affiliations.

The Evidence

  • Date and Time: March 2, 2019, approximately 2:58 a.m.
  • Location: Attached garage at 12 / 12A Greenwood Street, Dorchester, Massachusetts.
  • Victimology: Kendric Price, 32, a local youth mentor and non-profit founder with no known gang affiliations.
  • Ballistics: Acoustic reports and witness accounts indicate up to 15 rounds were fired at the scene; Price sustained fatal ballistic trauma.
  • Manner of Death: Homicide, as ruled by the Office of the Chief Medical Examiner.
  • Investigating Agency: Boston Police Department Homicide Unit.

Investigators urge individuals who were present at the Greenwood Street gathering to break the silence and assist in closing the case. Tips can be directed to the BPD Homicide Unit at 617-343-4470, or anonymously through the CrimeStoppers Tip Line at 1-800-494-TIPS.
