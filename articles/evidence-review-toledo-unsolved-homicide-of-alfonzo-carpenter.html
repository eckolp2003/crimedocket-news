Evidence Review: Toledo Unsolved Homicide of Alfonzo Carpenter

Toledo Police continue to seek forensic leads in the 2022 fatal shooting at the intersection of Berdan and Watson avenues.

Crime Docket March 26, 2026 516 words · 2 min read

A fatal gunshot wound sustained at the intersection of Berdan and Watson avenues on December 30, 2022, remains the central forensic focus in the Toledo unsolved homicide of Alfonzo Carpenter Jr. An August 2025 broadcast by WTVG’s I-TEAM highlighted the stalled local investigation, prompting renewed requests for material evidence from the public.

Crime Scene and Initial Dispatch

On December 30, 2022, shortly before 12:30 a.m., Toledo Police Department (TPD) officers were dispatched to West Toledo following a “person down” report. Upon arrival at the intersection, first responders located 25-year-old Alfonzo L. Carpenter Jr. suffering from at least one gunshot wound. Emergency medical personnel transported Carpenter to a local hospital, where he later succumbed to his injuries.

Because the initial dispatch was categorized as a medical emergency rather than an active shooter event, first responders prioritized immediate lifesaving measures. In such scenarios, the necessary medical triage and rapid transport of a victim can occasionally disrupt trace evidence at the immediate perimeter. Law enforcement records indicate the shooter fled the scene prior to police arrival.

To date, crime scene technicians and investigators have not publicly released specific ballistic data—such as casing calibers, bullet trajectory, or the suspected type of firearm utilized. Retaining this information as “holdback evidence” (details known only to the perpetrator and investigators) is a standard law enforcement procedure used to evaluate the veracity of future witness statements or suspect confessions. The fatal shooting was officially recorded as Toledo’s 65th homicide of 2022.

Jurisdictional Status and Records Review

An analysis of federal and judicial databases—specifically FBI.gov, the National Archives, and the CourtListener federal docket system—yields zero federal records pertaining to Carpenter’s death. This absence of federal documentation is standard protocol for unsolved local homicides.

Federal jurisdiction typically only triggers if a suspect crosses state lines to evade capture, if the crime involves a federal entity, or if civil rights litigation is filed in U.S. District Court. Consequently, the investigation remains strictly under the purview of local Ohio law enforcement.

Investigative Media Appeals

The case received renewed analytical focus on August 5, 2025, when WTVG (13abc Toledo) featured the investigation in their “I-TEAM Case Files” segment. Reporter Christina Williams highlighted the ongoing nature of the unsolved homicide, noting that cases without immediate suspect apprehension rely heavily on delayed witness testimony or newly processed forensic links, such as matches in the National Integrated Ballistic Information Network (NIBIN).

Authorities are directing all actionable intelligence to local investigators.

The Evidence

  • Incident Location: Intersection of Berdan Avenue and Watson Avenue, West Toledo, Ohio.
  • Time of Discovery: Initial dispatch received just prior to 12:30 a.m. on Friday, December 30, 2022.
  • Mechanism of Death: Gunshot wound(s); the victim succumbed to injuries post-hospital transport.
  • Suspect Status: Unknown; the perpetrator fled the scene before law enforcement secured the perimeter.
  • Jurisdictional Record: Toledo Police Department (classified as Toledo Homicide #65 of 2022); no federal warrants or dockets exist.

Anyone possessing digital evidence (such as dashboard camera or surveillance video from the area), ballistic information, or witness accounts is urged by local authorities to contact Toledo Crime Stoppers.
