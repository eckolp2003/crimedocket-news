Archival VHS Footage Solves 1990 William Arnold Newton Cold Case

A filmmaker's analysis of 1990s award show footage identified a suspect, leading to an on-camera prison confession in the 35-year-old dismemberment homicide.

Crime Docket March 26, 2026 818 words · 4 min read

The review of archival VHS footage from an early 1990s adult film industry award show has provided the critical lead required to resolve the 35-year-old unsolved homicide of William Arnold Newton. Following the visual identification of a suspect in the background of the tape, documentary filmmaker Rachel Mason secured an on-camera confession from DarraLynn Madden, an inmate currently serving a life sentence for an unrelated homicide, effectively closing the West Hollywood cold case.

The evidentiary breakthrough, detailed in Mason’s SXSW-premiering documentary My Brother’s Killer and recently reported by The Advocate, highlights the emerging forensic utility of marginalized media archives in resolving long-dormant cases.

Archival Evidence and Visual Identification

Because mainstream media outlets rarely documented LGBTQ+ life in the late 1980s and early 1990s, adult film archives serve as a rare, contemporary visual record. While researching the case, Mason collaborated with queer archivists and cold-case detectives to analyze surviving media from the era.

The pivotal associative evidence (material that links a suspect to a crime or victim) emerged during a frame-by-frame review of a VHS tape recording a gay adult industry memorial award show. In the footage, Mason visually identified the suspected killer walking on stage. This identification placed the suspect within the victim’s immediate professional orbit during the exact chronological window of the crime, providing investigators and the filmmaker with a specific individual to trace.

The 1990 West Hollywood Crime Scene

William Arnold Newton, who operated in the adult film industry under the pseudonym Billy London, was murdered in October 1990. The crime scene data was localized to West Hollywood, where law enforcement discovered plastic garbage bags containing Newton’s dismembered head and feet in a commercial dumpster. Due to the specific post-mortem mutilation, the case was unofficially designated the “gay Black Dahlia” within the local community.

At the time of the discovery, forensic technology was severely limited. Routine DNA profiling (STR analysis) was not yet standard practice in municipal crime labs, and the marginalization of the victim’s demographic resulted in minimal investigative resource allocation. Furthermore, the localized recovery of only the head and feet severely limited the medical examiner’s ability to conduct a comprehensive autopsy to determine the precise sequence of sharp force trauma or the primary cause of death.

Suspect Modus Operandi and Confession

The visual identification from the VHS tape led Mason to DarraLynn Madden, a transgender woman who had committed the murder prior to her transition. According to investigative data sourced by The Advocate, Madden’s Modus Operandi (method of operation) was facilitated by her access to the victim’s community combined with a documented history of hate-motivated violence.

During the 1990 timeline, Madden was operating as an adult film actor and sex worker while maintaining active associations with a group of neo-Nazi skinheads. The investigation revealed that Madden encountered and killed Newton during an active hunt for targets with this hate group.

Tracking Madden’s subsequent criminal history, Mason located her in a maximum-security prison, where she is currently serving a life sentence for a separate homicide and additional felony convictions. Following a period of written correspondence, Mason secured an in-depth, on-camera interview wherein Madden provided a full confession to Newton’s murder, detailing specifics of the crime that aligned with the 1990 forensic findings.

Exceptional Clearance and Case Disposition

Despite the recorded confession, prosecutors will not formally file homicide charges against Madden for Newton’s death. Law enforcement agencies frequently close cases under exceptional clearance (an administrative classification used when an agency has identified the offender and gathered enough evidence to support an arrest, but outside circumstances prevent formal prosecution).

In this instance, the lack of surviving physical evidence from 1990 prevents the corroboration required for a trial. Spoliation of evidence (the loss, destruction, or significant degradation of biological material over 35 years) means no physical link remains to support the confession in a court of law. Additionally, because Madden is already serving a maximum-security life sentence, prosecutorial agencies rarely expend municipal resources on secondary trials that will not alter the inmate’s custodial status.

However, the definitive confession achieved the primary goal of the investigation: providing closure to Newton’s surviving family and his partner, Marc Rabins, who was excluded as a person of interest following Madden’s admission.

The Evidence

  • Victim Identity: William Arnold Newton (alias Billy London), targeted in October 1990.
  • Crime Scene Recovery: Remains (head and feet) discovered in plastic bags within a West Hollywood dumpster.
  • Key Evidentiary Lead: Suspect visually identified via archival VHS footage of a 1990s adult industry memorial event.
  • Suspect Identification: DarraLynn Madden, who was simultaneously operating within the adult film industry and associating with violent neo-Nazi factions at the time of the offense.
  • Case Resolution: Madden provided a full on-camera confession from a maximum-security prison.
  • Legal Status: Case is effectively closed via exceptional clearance; no formal charges will be filed due to historical evidence degradation and the suspect’s existing life sentence.
