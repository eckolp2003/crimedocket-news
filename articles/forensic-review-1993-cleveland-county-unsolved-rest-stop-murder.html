Forensic Review: 1993 Cleveland County Unsolved Rest Stop Murder

Ligature marks and close-range ballistics define the evidence file in Cleveland County's oldest active cold case.

Crime Docket March 26, 2026 893 words · 4 min read

Ligature marks and a close-range gunshot wound to the chest form the evidentiary core of Cleveland County’s oldest active homicide investigation. The crime scene—a locked maintenance utility room at an Interstate 85 Southbound rest area—yielded limited physical evidence, leaving the Cleveland County Sheriff’s Office with a complex cold case that has spanned more than three decades.

On the morning of Sunday, September 26, 1993, first responders discovered the body of 39-year-old North Carolina Department of Transportation (NCDOT) employee Charles Alvin “Chuck” Porter. Recent coverage by forensic journalism series WBTV True Crime Carolinas in March 2026 has renewed focus on the physical and acoustic evidence of the case, aiming to generate fresh leads from the public.

Crime Scene Analysis at the I-85 Rest Stop

Forensic reconstruction of the morning indicates that the perpetrators utilized environmental factors to delay discovery of the victim. Porter was working a scheduled maintenance shift at the rest area. According to case files from the Cleveland County Sheriff’s Office, a coworker noted that Porter routinely kept the heavy utility room door propped open while working inside.

Following the incident, the coworker found the prop removed and the door closed and locked from the inside. This suggests the suspects actively secured the perimeter of the primary crime scene before fleeing, a tactical decision that delayed the coworker’s entry and gave the perpetrators a wider window for egress. Upon unlocking the door with a designated key, the coworker discovered Porter deceased on the floor.

Investigators documented that Porter had been bound prior to his death. The presence of restraints (ligatures) in an active workplace setting strongly indicates the perpetrators sought compliance, leading investigators to classify the primary motive as an interrupted or escalated robbery. Given that Porter had no documented enemies and was described by acquaintances as highly agreeable, investigators ruled out targeted interpersonal violence early in the inquiry.

Ballistics and Acoustic Discrepancies

Medical examiners determined the cause of death to be a fatal gunshot wound to the chest, specifically targeting the heart. The proximity of the weapon discharge—noted as extremely close range—supports the theory of a confined-space encounter inside the utility room. The trajectory and range indicate Porter was likely shot immediately after being bound, resulting in near-instantaneous death.

An analysis of witness statements reveals a notable discrepancy in acoustic perception, a common forensic variable in environments with high ambient noise. Interstate 85 generates significant decibel levels from vehicular traffic and heavy machinery.

Porter’s coworker, stationed nearby, reported hearing a loud “pop” but initially attributed the sound to an electrical malfunction within the maintenance facility. Conversely, travelers positioned outside the facility reported hearing two distinct gunshots. This acoustic variance suggests that the structural dampening of the utility room walls, combined with the masking effect of highway traffic, isolated the auditory evidence. Investigators have not publicly disclosed whether ballistic forensics recovered one or two spent casings at the scene, though the dual-gunshot eyewitness reports suggest the weapon was fired multiple times.

Suspect Profiling and Egress Tactics

The eyewitness testimony provided critical data regarding the suspects’ flight path and premeditation. Travelers in the rest area observed two individuals fleeing the immediate vicinity of the utility room following the gunfire.

According to the Cleveland County Sheriff’s Office, witnesses described the suspects as two young Black males, with one explicitly observed carrying a firearm. The suspects fled to a dark-colored Volkswagen in the parking lot.

Forensically, the positioning of the getaway vehicle is highly significant: witnesses noted the Volkswagen had been backed into a parking space. In tactical crime scene analysis, reverse-parking (combat parking) is an indicator of premeditation, allowing for rapid, unobstructed forward acceleration during an escape. The vehicle sped away from the rest stop, merging directly onto Interstate 85 Southbound.

Law enforcement utilized the eyewitness descriptions to generate composite sketches of the two suspects. Despite widespread circulation in 1993, these forensic sketches did not yield actionable identifications, highlighting the difficulty of solving transient-location homicides where perpetrators quickly cross jurisdictional or state lines.

The Evidence

  • Location: Interstate 85 Southbound rest stop utility/maintenance room, Cleveland County, North Carolina.
  • Victimology: 39-year-old NCDOT maintenance employee with no known enemies; the incident occurred one day before he and his wife were scheduled to move into a newly constructed home.
  • Crime Scene Manipulation: Suspects removed a door prop and locked the utility room door upon exiting, delaying discovery.
  • Restraints: Victim was tied up by the perpetrators prior to the fatal injury.
  • Ballistics: Cause of death was a close-range gunshot wound to the chest/heart.
  • Acoustics: Outdoor witnesses reported two distinct gunshots; an indoor coworker heard a single “pop” masked by structural and ambient noise.
  • Suspect Egress: Two young Black males fled in a dark-colored Volkswagen that had been reverse-parked for rapid departure toward I-85 Southbound.

Active Cold Case Status

The 1993 murder of Charles Porter remains an open and active investigation. Decades of cold case analysis demonstrate that shifting personal allegiances over time frequently lead to new informant data. As noted by WBTV True Crime Carolinas, investigators are seeking any fragmented information that may corroborate existing physical evidence.

The Cleveland County Sheriff’s Office requests that anyone with knowledge regarding the dark-colored Volkswagen, the composite sketches, or the events of September 26, 1993, contact their tip line at 704-484-4822. Anonymous tips can also be routed through 704-484-4788 or 704-484-4787.
