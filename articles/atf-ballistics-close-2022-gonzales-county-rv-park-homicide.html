ATF Ballistics Close 2022 Gonzales County RV Park Homicide

A forensic interview and ballistic matching confirm a seven-year-old committed the unsolved shooting of Brandon O'Quinn Rasberry, precluding prosecution under Texas law.

Crime Docket March 24, 2026 1144 words · 5 min read

Ballistic analysis conducted by the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF) has resolved a two-year-old cold case in Texas, matching two spent 9mm shell casings recovered from a 2022 crime scene to a firearm recently pawned in Seguin, Texas. The forensic confirmation corroborates a voluntary confession obtained during an April 2024 child advocacy center interview, identifying the perpetrator of the Gonzales County RV park homicide as a child who was seven years old at the time of the shooting.

The findings conclude the investigation into the death of 32-year-old Brandon O’Quinn Rasberry, whose case sat dormant for 24 months due to an absence of forensic leads. However, strict statutory limitations within the Texas penal system dictate that the homicide will be closed without murder charges.

Crime Scene Forensics and Initial Investigation

On January 18, 2022, deputies from the Gonzales County Sheriff’s Office (GCSO) responded to a welfare check at the Lazy J RV Park in Nixon, Texas, located at 85 Wild Meadow. An employer at a local poultry processing facility requested the check after Rasberry failed to report for scheduled shifts. The property owner entered the trailer and discovered the deceased.

Crime scene investigators documented the scene, noting that the RV was unlocked and showed no signs of forced entry or a struggle. An autopsy subsequently performed by the Travis County Medical Examiner’s Office in Austin, Texas, confirmed the cause of death as a single gunshot wound to the head. The medical examiner also documented minor graze wounds to two of the victim’s fingers, indicating the trajectory of the projectile.

Physical evidence collection at the scene yielded two spent 9mm shell casings. Trajectory analysis determined that one round resulted in the fatal injury, while the second round was discharged into the RV’s couch. No firearm was recovered at the scene.

Digital Data and Geolocation Dead Ends

In the absence of eyewitnesses or an identified suspect, GCSO investigators utilized digital forensics to attempt to generate leads. Law enforcement executed search warrants for cellular device extraction and geolocation data associated with Rasberry’s phone.

Investigators additionally sought cell tower dumps (a technique where law enforcement requests data from telecommunications companies to identify all mobile devices that connected to specific cell towers during a specific timeframe). However, the cellular data provided no actionable intelligence. With no known interpersonal conflicts in the victim’s background and no forensic ties to a suspect, the case stalled.

The 2024 Forensic Interview and Confession

The investigative breakthrough originated not from physical evidence, but from an unrelated threat investigation. On April 12, 2024, administrators at the Nixon-Smiley Consolidated Independent School District (CISD) contacted GCSO dispatchers regarding a 10-year-old student who made a terroristic threat to assault and kill another student on a school bus.

Following standard protocol for juvenile threats, the child was taken to a local child advocacy center for a forensic interview. A forensic interview is a structured, trauma-informed questioning technique utilized by trained specialists to gather factual information from minors while minimizing psychological distress and preventing suggestibility.

During this recorded interview, the 10-year-old suspect voluntarily disclosed details regarding the unsolved 2022 homicide of Brandon Rasberry. According to the GCSO, the child provided specific crime scene details that had not been released to the public.

The minor stated that on January 16, 2022—when he was seven years old—he was visiting his grandfather, who resided in an RV lot near Rasberry’s trailer. The child confessed to removing a loaded 9mm pistol from the glove compartment of his grandfather’s truck, entering Rasberry’s unlocked RV, and discharging the weapon twice while the victim slept. The child then returned the firearm to the vehicle.

When investigators inquired about a motive, the child stated he had no prior relationship with Rasberry, had never spoken to him, and held no grievances against him; he had merely observed the man walking the grounds of the RV park earlier that afternoon.

ATF Ballistic Analysis Confirms the Weapon

To corroborate the confession, GCSO investigators tracked the chain of custody of the grandfather’s firearm. Records indicated the grandfather had legally pawned the 9mm pistol at a shop in Seguin, Texas, sometime after the 2022 homicide.

Law enforcement recovered the firearm and submitted it to the ATF San Antonio Field Office. On April 17, 2024, ATF forensic technicians conducted a comparative ballistic analysis. By test-firing the recovered weapon and utilizing comparison microscopy, analysts examined the microscopic striations (toolmarks) left on the test casings. The ATF confirmed that the unique toolmarks on the weapon’s breech face and firing pin definitively matched the two spent 9mm shell casings collected from the Lazy J RV Park crime scene in 2022.

Texas Penal Code Section 8.07 and Legal Limitations

Despite the forensic corroboration and the suspect’s detailed confession, local prosecutors are legally barred from filing homicide charges in this case.

Texas Penal Code Section 8.07, which governs Age Affecting Criminal Responsibility, establishes a strict statutory floor for criminal culpability. Under the law, a child cannot be prosecuted or convicted for any criminal offense committed before their 10th birthday. Because the suspect was seven years old (one week shy of his eighth birthday) on January 16, 2022, he maintains absolute legal immunity from criminal prosecution regarding Rasberry’s death.

The suspect currently faces a terroristic threat charge stemming solely from the April 2024 school bus incident. Following his confession, he was placed on a 72-hour emergency psychiatric detention and transferred into the custody of the Gonzales County Juvenile Probation Department.

According to local reports by WOAI, Rasberry’s surviving family members are currently advocating for legislative reform. The proposed amendment, colloquially termed “Brandon O’Quinn Rasberry’s Law,” seeks to alter state statutes to allow for juvenile accountability in violent crimes, regardless of the offender’s age at the time of the incident.

The Evidence

  • The Crime Scene: Brandon O’Quinn Rasberry (32) was found deceased on January 18, 2022, inside his unlocked trailer at the Lazy J RV Park in Nixon, Texas.
  • Autopsy Findings: The Travis County Medical Examiner confirmed the cause of death was a single gunshot wound to the head, accompanied by minor graze wounds to the fingers.
  • Ballistics: Two spent 9mm shell casings were recovered at the scene—one fatal, one lodged in the couch.
  • Digital Forensics: Cell phone extractions and tower dumps conducted in 2022 yielded no actionable leads.
  • The Confession: During an April 2024 forensic interview regarding an unrelated school bus threat, a 10-year-old child confessed to taking his grandfather’s 9mm pistol and shooting Rasberry in 2022, when the child was seven.
  • Forensic Confirmation: On April 17, 2024, the ATF San Antonio Field Office matched the 2022 crime scene shell casings to the grandfather’s pawned 9mm pistol via toolmark analysis.
  • Legal Resolution: Due to Texas Penal Code Section 8.07, individuals under age 10 lack criminal culpability, precluding any murder charges for the 2022 homicide.
